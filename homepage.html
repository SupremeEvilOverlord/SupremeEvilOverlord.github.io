Artist Statement

Let’s change the perspective with how we see life. What keeps me coming back to the studio, the sketchbook, the laptop is to tell a story for those who are misunderstood and unheard in this society, who see the world in a different way from others. Why is what all my viewers ask once they see each piece in fascination, the surprise of what I think and feel in my artwork. The best way to describe my artwork is as one person said “these were hard for me to look at, but this is what people portray them as” making me know that my viewers and critics understand the trauma, the emotion, and the humanity of my artwork without alot of explanations. So why don’t we change the perspective by bringing light into the truth and meaning of our everyday lives, while the other is to set be set free and release us from the fears that hold us back
